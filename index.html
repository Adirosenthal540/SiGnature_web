SiGnature: Explicit Motion Diffusion
for Stylized Semantic Gesture Generation

Adi Rosenthal, Tomer Koren, Nadav Shaked, Doron Friedman* , Ariel Shamir*
Reichman University
SCA 2026

Abstract

While recent advances in co-speech gesture generation have achieved impressive rhythmic synchronization, synthesizing gestures that are both semantically meaningful and faithful to a speaker's unique non-verbal style remains an open challenge. Semantic gestures, such as iconic shapes or deictic pointing, are statistically sparse, making them difficult to learn effectively within standard generative models.

We present SiGnature, a framework for Stylized and Semantic Gesture generation that reconciles precise semantic control with high-fidelity style preservation.

Unlike prevalent methods that rely on entangled latent representations, SiGnature operates in an explicit joint-rotation space. This design enables our core contribution, Joint Motion Integration (JMI), a training-free inference mechanism capable of injecting any external motion sequence, particularly in-the-wild semantic gestures, directly into the diffusion process. JMI automatically identifies the specific "active joints" conveying a semantic action and injects them into the generation, while relying on the diffusion backbone to synthesize the remaining body dynamics, including posture and flow, in accordance with the pre-learned style of the target speaker. This allows for the plug-and-play integration of arbitrary motions, including complex semantic gestures, without retraining or introducing the "Frankenstein" artifacts typical of cut-and-paste methods.

Extensive experiments and perceptual studies demonstrate that SiGnature offers superior semantic motion control while maintaining smooth and natural co-speech gesture generation and preserving the distinct characteristics of the speaker, thereby outperforming state-of-the-art baselines.

Approach

Semantic gestures (pointing, iconic shapes, a thumbs-down) are rare in training data, so standard co-speech models wash them out or snap them to generic patterns. SiGnature keeps each speaker's personal motion style from a learned diffusion backbone, then surgically injects the semantic gesture only into the joints that carry its meaning, with no retraining and none of the "Frankenstein" artifacts of cut-and-paste methods.

Part I — Multimodal Diffusion Backbone. We generate co-speech body motion directly in SMPL-X rotation space using a diffusion model. Starting from Gaussian noise, the model iteratively denoises a motion sequence, conditioned on the speaker's audio, transcript, and a global semantic embedding. Working in explicit rotation space (rather than a compressed latent space) gives us smooth, jitter-free motion and precise, per-joint control.

Part II — Joint Motion Integration (JMI). At inference time, we augment the pretrained backbone with a semantic gesture clip. A Find Active Joints module automatically identifies which joints (and frames) carry the gesture's meaning. JMI then injects only those joint trajectories into the backbone's prediction at every diffusion step, while the backbone freely synthesizes the rest of the body in the target speaker's own style. The result enforces the intended semantic gesture while preserving the speaker's posture, flow, and personality - all training-free.

Style Preservation

Style Preservation Style is captured implicitly by fine-tuning a separate personalized diffusion model per identity. The table below evaluates our framework's ability to preserve target speaker identity during semantic injection, preventing the speaker's distinctive signature style from being overwritten. Columns are the ground-truth (GT) motions and rows are our generated results; lower is better. The consistently low diagonal entries confirm that SiGnature synthesizes the target speaker's gestural style, while the comparatively high off-diagonal entries show the model does not suffer from style leakage or collapse into an "average" speaker. The last row (Source SeG) reveals that the raw semantic gestures originate from a distinct, out-of-distribution style — contrasting these high source scores with our low diagonal scores (e.g. Sophie: 5.19 → 1.87) shows that JMI avoids naive motion-pasting, instead pulling the integrated semantic gesture back into the target speaker's specific stylistic manifold. Co-Speech Backbone vs. State of the Art

Before any semantic injection, our diffusion backbone alone produces co-speech gestures that are markedly smoother than latent-space baselines. By generating motion directly in explicit rotation space, we achieve the lowest jerk and acceleration while keeping the highest motion diversity — a property we call active smoothness: motion that is physically clean yet highly dynamic.
